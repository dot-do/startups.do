  problem:
    - >-

    - >-
      Many nuts lack liquid exchange-traded futures; hedging is done via
      supplier forwards, options on FX, and inventory strategies—leaving teams
      with ad hoc, spreadsheet-based risk management and limited backtesting.
    - >-
      Weather shocks (California rainfall/frost for almonds; Turkish frost for
      hazelnuts; monsoons for cashews/peanuts) can change supply outlooks
      quickly, but alerts aren’t connected to actionable playbooks.
    - >-
      Supplier risk (recalls, credit, labor disruptions, export policy shifts)
      is opaque, causing last-minute RFQs and premium buys.
    - >-
      RFQ cycles are slow and inconsistent (days to weeks), with unclear
      specifications (grade, moisture, aflatoxin, crop year, Incoterms),
      reducing supplier response quality and raising prices.
    - >-
      Finance lacks clear visibility into hedge coverage, price variance vs
      benchmark, and realized savings; no single source of truth ties decisions
      to outcomes and audit/compliance needs.
    - >-
      Cross-commodity substitution and correlation (e.g., almond vs cashew
      kernels, peanut vs soybean oil) are not quantified to inform purchasing
      strategy.
