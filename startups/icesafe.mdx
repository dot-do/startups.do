  problem:
    - >-
      Paper/spreadsheet logs for CCP checks, calibrations, and sanitation are
      error-prone, hard to trend, and costly to maintain (typical plant spends
      6–12 hours/week on documentation and filing).
    - >-
      Missed or late CCP checks (e.g., sanitizer concentration, water quality,
      UV status) lead to non-conformances; many plants average 70–80% on-time
      checks without automated reminders/escalations.
    - >-
      Manual range validation is inconsistent; operators may record out-of-range
      values without triggering immediate corrective action, increasing risk of
      unsafe product release.
    - >-
      Audit preparation is burdensome (8–20 hours per audit to compile logs,
      calibrations, sanitation records, training, and lab COAs) and often
      incomplete.
    - >-
      Calibration records (thermometers, TDS meters, chlorine test kits, scales)
      are scattered, increasing audit findings and downtime from uncalibrated
      instruments.
    - >-
      Lack of real-time visibility for QA/Plant Managers across multiple
      lines/shifts/sites hampers early detection and trend analysis (e.g.,
      rising turbidity, recurring sanitation pre-op failures).
    - >-
      Generic food safety tools lack ice-specific CCP templates and OEM
      integrations (Vogt, Hoshizaki, Scotsman), forcing heavy customization and
      inconsistent execution.
    - >-
      Corrective actions are ad hoc; root cause, disposition, and verification
      steps are not standardized, elongating closure times and risking repeat
      issues.
    - >-
      Training drift and turnover cause SOP/HACCP deviations; onboarding new
      operators onto compliant routines is slow without guided digital
      workflows.
    - >-
      Data integrity (audit trails, e-signatures, version control) is inadequate
      to satisfy FSMA expectations and GFSI schemes (SQF/BRCGS), causing
      avoidable non-conformances.
